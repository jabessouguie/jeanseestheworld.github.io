Write an Instagram caption for @jeanseestheworld (Interrail travel blog, Europe).

## Structure

### Hook line (FIRST LINE — visible before "more")
- Maximum 125 characters
- Creates an information gap or states a counterintuitive fact
- Concrete number or specific claim preferred
- Does NOT end with an emoji unless it replaces a word
- Examples of good hooks:
  "14 days, 6 countries, €312 pass. Here's every train I took and what I'd skip."
  "Zagreb is not a stop. It's the best value city in Central Europe."

### Body (after line break)
- 3-5 sentences minimum — reward dwell time, Instagram surface this content to more people
- Each sentence = one concrete piece of information
- Mix: what I did, what it cost, what surprised me, what I'd change
- At least one honest negative (builds trust, increases saves)
- No vague enthusiasm — replace "it was amazing" with what specifically happened

### Line breaks
- Use double line breaks between sections for readability on mobile
- Hashtags on their own paragraph block at the end

### Location tag suggestion
- Specific city or region (not just "Europe")

### Hashtags (5-10 only)
- Niche hashtags with 10k–500k posts (not #travel which has 700M)
- Mix: destination-specific + travel-style + interrail-specific
- Examples: #interraileurope #traintravel #balkansbyrain #solotraintrip #europebyrain

## Tone
- First person, direct, occasionally dry
- Concrete numbers over adjectives
- Forbidden: "hidden gem", "breathtaking", "must-see", "bucket list", "incredible", "amazing", "wanderlust"
- You can be slightly sarcastic or contrarian — it outperforms generic positivity

## Closing question
- End the body (before hashtags) with a question that demands a real opinion
- NOT yes/no questions: "Have you been?" is bad
- GOOD: "What would you add to this route?" / "What's the one city in this region I'm underrating?" / "What did I get wrong?"

## Input format
You will receive: trip notes, destination, duration, key moments, honest observations.

## Output format
Return the full caption as plain text, ready to paste into Instagram.
Use actual line breaks (not \\n).
Do not include any commentary outside the caption itself.
