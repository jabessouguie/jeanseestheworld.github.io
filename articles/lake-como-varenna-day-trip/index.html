Lake Como from Milan: Do It Better Than the €60 Guided Tour

Train to Varenna, ferry to Bellagio, lunch on the lake — for about €60 total instead of €120–180

Every travel desk in Milan offers a Lake Como day trip for €120–180 per person: minibus to Como, boat cruise, lunch included, back by 18:00. The same day — a better version of it — costs about €60 if you take the Trenord train directly to Varenna. The difference is €60–120 in your pocket, no group pace, and the freedom to stay for the 19:00 light on the water instead of being back at the hotel by teatime. The train leaves Milano Centrale every hour from 06:20, takes 64 minutes, and costs €7.40 one way. The ferry across to Bellagio is €5. You need one lunch reservation if you're going on a weekend. That's the entire logistics of this day.

Seat tip

After Lecco, the line hugs the eastern shore of the lake. Sit on the left side of the train (facing the direction of travel) for the lake view. The stretch from Lecco to Varenna — about 20 minutes — is the payoff: cliffs on one side, water on the other.

Buy the ticket at the Trenord machines in Milano Centrale (ground floor, departures hall) or via the Trenord app. No advance booking needed — regional ticket, fixed price regardless of when you buy. If you buy a paper ticket, validate it at the yellow stamping machines before boarding.

Varenna: start here, not Bellagio

Varenna is where the train stops. It's also the right place to start the day — quieter than Bellagio, with a proper village texture. The main lakefront drag is the Passeggiata degli Innamorati, a narrow walkway built over the water's edge, connecting the ferry dock to the southern end of town. It takes 10 minutes to walk end to end and costs nothing.

If you want a structured stop: Villa Monastero is a lakeside villa with formal terraced gardens and a small museum inside. Entry is around €10. The gardens are the draw — descending in terraces toward the water, with citrus trees and old-growth wisteria.

Practical note

The ferry dock in Varenna is a 5-minute walk downhill from the train station. Follow the signs for "Battello" or just walk toward the water. You cannot miss it.

The ferry across: Varenna to Bellagio (and Menaggio)

The Navigazione Laghi ferry connects Varenna, Bellagio and Menaggio in a triangular route. The crossing from Varenna to Bellagio takes about 15 minutes. The ticket is around €5 per person one way.

  • Bellagio: the most photographed village on the lake. Narrow cobblestone lanes, small shops, and the view down the lake split. Busy in July and August, considerably calmer in May, June, September. The waterfront is pleasant; the village is 20 minutes to walk through properly.
  • Menaggio: the western shore option on the same ferry. Less known, longer lakefront promenade, fewer tourists. A good choice if Bellagio feels too crowded or you want somewhere to sit quietly for an hour.

The ferry runs every 30–60 minutes depending on season. Buy tickets at the dock — cash or card. No advance booking needed.

Lunch: book ahead on weekends

Two reliable options in Varenna in the €20–30 per person range (starter or pasta + main + water/house wine):

  • Il Cavatappi — small trattoria on a side street, local fish from the lake, pasta made in-house. Quieter than the waterfront places. Book via phone or their website if coming on a Saturday or Sunday.
  • Quattro Pass al Lago — lakeside terrace, more relaxed atmosphere, slightly larger menu. The view compensates for slightly higher prices on the drinks.

If you're in Bellagio for lunch, the same price range applies — waterfront tables cost a premium of about €5–8 per person on the bill. Eating one street back cuts that cost.

A comparable guided tour from Milan costs €120–180 per person, typically includes a less flexible schedule and group-pace sightseeing. The train+ferry version gives you the same lake, the same villages, and 6–8 hours on your own terms.

Last train warning

The last Trenord train from Varenna-Esino to Milan departs at 21:35. Arrive at the platform at least 20 minutes early — on summer evenings it fills quickly. If you miss it, the next option is a combination of local buses and a long wait. Set an alarm for 21:00 while you're at dinner.

Skip: Como city

Como city sits at the very southern tip of the western arm of the lake. It's reachable from Milan on a different Trenord line (45 minutes, €5.40) but the lake views there are not the panoramic central-lake views most people have in mind. Getting from Como to Bellagio or Varenna adds roughly 2 hours of extra travel via boat or bus. For a day trip focused on seeing the lake, Varenna is the correct starting point.

Frequently asked questions

Which is the best train to take from Milan to Lake Como?

Take the Trenord regional train from Milano Centrale to Varenna-Esino. It runs roughly every hour from 06:20 to 21:35 and takes about 64 minutes. The one-way ticket costs €7.40. This beats going to Como city, which is on the opposite arm of the lake and requires an additional 2-hour journey to reach the central lake area.

Why go to Varenna instead of Como or Bellagio?

Varenna is directly accessible by train (64 minutes from Milan), has fewer day-trippers than Bellagio, and is a pleasant town in its own right — with the Passeggiata degli Innamorati lakeside walkway and Villa Monastero gardens. From Varenna you can take the 15-minute ferry to Bellagio. Going directly to Bellagio is harder: it requires a bus or car from Como, or a longer ferry ride.

How much does the ferry cost on Lake Como?

The ferry from Varenna to Bellagio costs approximately €5 per person each way (€10 return). The crossing takes about 15 minutes. Ferries run frequently during the day — every 30–60 minutes depending on the season. The same ferry also connects to Menaggio on the western shore.

What time is the last train back from Varenna to Milan?

The last Trenord train from Varenna-Esino to Milano Centrale departs at approximately 21:35. Arrive at the platform at least 20 minutes early — it fills up quickly in the evening, especially on summer weekends. Missing it means waiting over an hour for the next option or finding alternative transport.

Is it worth going to Como city from Milan?

Not as part of a day trip to Lake Como. Como city sits at the southern tip of the western arm of the lake — the views are not representative of what makes Lake Como distinctive, and reaching the central lake area (Bellagio, Varenna) from Como adds 2+ hours of travel. Take the train directly to Varenna-Esino instead.
