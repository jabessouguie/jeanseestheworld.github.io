Eurostar Paris or London to Amsterdam: How to Get the Best Price

3h20 from Paris city centre to Amsterdam city centre. Here's how to not pay three times the base fare.

Paris / London → Amsterdam 3h20 (Paris) / 3h52 (London) Year-round

I've taken the Eurostar from Paris to Amsterdam twice. The first time I booked 3 weeks ahead and paid €149 each way. The second time I booked 6 months ahead and paid €44 each way — same class, same journey. That €105 difference for a 5-minute difference in planning is the entire reason this article exists. The Eurostar pricing model rewards early booking more aggressively than almost any other train in Europe, and there are a few specific tactics that make a real difference: booking at the 6-month mark, filling in your age profile, choosing the right travel days, and understanding what the Interrail reservation fee actually covers. Here's what I know.

The 6-month rule: when to book

Eurostar releases seats exactly 6 months before departure. The cheapest Standard seats — the "Snap" fares that start as low as €44 on some routes — sell out within days of release, sometimes hours for popular departure times. The pricing structure from there:

Booking window Typical price range (Standard)
6 months ahead (release day) €44–€79
3–6 months ahead €79–€120
1–3 months ahead €100–€180
Under 1 month €149–€250+

These are not precise guarantees — demand, season, and departure time all affect the final number. But the pattern holds: prices roughly triple from the 6-month booking to a 3-week booking. Set a calendar reminder for 6 months before your travel date and check the Eurostar website on that exact day.

Tip

The 6-month window opens at midnight UK time. Booking in the first hour of the release day gives you access to the full cheap-seat allocation before other passengers clear it.

Youth and senior fares: fill in your profile

Eurostar offers reduced fares for under-26 travellers and over-60 travellers. These fares are not shown by default. They only appear if you have a Eurostar account with your date of birth saved in your profile.

How to unlock age-based fares

  1. Create a free account at eurostar.com
  2. Go to your profile settings and enter your date of birth
  3. When searching for tickets while logged in, the system automatically applies eligible youth or senior rates at checkout

The discount varies but is typically 10–25% off the standard adult fare. On a €100 ticket, that's €10–25 saved for a 2-minute account setup. Do this before you search — not after you've already seen adult prices.

Cheapest travel days: Tuesday and Wednesday

Eurostar prices fluctuate significantly by day of the week. The business travel pattern creates Friday and Sunday evening price peaks — these are the most expensive departures. The cheapest departures are consistently Tuesday and Wednesday mornings.

Day Relative price
Tuesday morning Lowest
Wednesday morning Low
Monday Medium
Thursday Medium
Friday afternoon/evening High
Sunday evening High

If you're planning a Netherlands Interrail trip and have flexibility on start dates, arriving in Amsterdam on a Tuesday morning instead of a Friday evening can save €40–60 on the Eurostar leg alone.

Train vs flying: the honest comparison

The flight from Paris to Amsterdam takes 1 hour 15 minutes in the air. That number is used constantly to argue that flying is faster. Here's what that comparison actually looks like door to door:

Eurostar (Paris–Amsterdam) Flight (CDG–AMS)
Travel time 3h20 1h15
Arrive at departure point 30–45 min before 2h before
City centre → departure point 15 min (Paris Nord is central) 35–45 min (CDG transfer)
Arrival → city centre 0 min (Amsterdam Centraal is city centre) 20–30 min (Schiphol train)
Total door to door ~4h15 ~4h45–5h15
Luggage in hold No — bring what you like Extra cost
Work/read during journey Yes — table and Wi-Fi Limited

The train is comparable or faster city centre to city centre. And on the train, those 3h20 are usable time — you sit at a table, you can work, you can read, you arrive without checked baggage fees. It's not slower. It's a different kind of time.

Interrail pass and Eurostar: what you need to know

An Interrail Global Pass is valid on Eurostar — the train company accepts the pass. However, a mandatory seat reservation is required and this is separate from the pass. Eurostar does not allow pass holders to board without a reservation, even with a valid pass.

Eurostar reservation fees for Interrail pass holders (2025)

  • Standard class, off-peak: €6
  • Standard class, peak: €25–35
  • Standard Premier: €35+

Book the reservation at eurostar.com (select "I have a Rail Pass" at checkout) or through the Rail Europe website. The Rail Planner app does not handle Eurostar reservations — you must go directly to Eurostar or an authorised agent.

Important

Eurostar reservations for pass holders are limited in number and can sell out. Book the reservation at the same time you confirm your travel dates — don't leave it until the week before. The reservation fee is low, but availability at peak times can be genuinely tight.

Is the Interrail pass worth using for Eurostar?

If you already have a pass, yes — a €6 reservation beats buying a full ticket. If you're debating whether to buy a pass specifically to use on Eurostar, the maths rarely works in the pass's favour for a single Eurostar journey. The pass value comes from multiple legs across multiple countries, not from a single route.

Standard vs Standard Premier: is the upgrade worth it?

Standard Premier on Eurostar includes a meal served at your seat, wider seats (2+1 layout vs 2+2), and access to the Business Premier Lounge at St Pancras or Paris Nord before boarding. Standard Premier costs approximately €80–150 more than Standard on a Paris–Amsterdam route.

My take: Standard is fine. The seat is comfortable. The tables are usable. There are plug sockets at seats (not all Eurostar trainsets — check). The meal in Standard Premier is not exceptional and the price difference is substantial. The lounge access at St Pancras is genuinely nice if you arrive at the station 90+ minutes early — it's calm and well-equipped. If you're arriving with 45 minutes to spare, the lounge isn't worth the premium.

Skip Premier unless you specifically want the lounge experience or the meal service.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I book Eurostar to Amsterdam?

Book 6 months in advance. Eurostar releases seats 6 months before departure and the cheapest Standard tickets sell out within days of release. Prices typically double after 3 months and can triple in the final month. If you know your travel date, book as soon as the 6-month window opens.

How long is the Eurostar from Paris to Amsterdam?

Paris Gare du Nord to Amsterdam Centraal takes 3 hours 20 minutes. This is city centre to city centre — no airport transfers, no security queues beyond a quick Eurostar passport check. Compare that to flying: 1h15 in the air plus 2 hours at the airport each end plus city transfers, easily totalling 5+ hours door to door.

Does Interrail cover the Eurostar to Amsterdam?

An Interrail pass is valid on Eurostar but a mandatory seat reservation is still required. Eurostar reservations for pass holders cost €6 in Standard class off-peak and up to €35 in peak. The reservation fee must be booked through Eurostar's website or an authorised agent — not through the Rail Planner app.

Are there youth or senior discounts on Eurostar?

Yes, but they only appear if you create a Eurostar account and fill in your date of birth. Youth fares (under 26) and senior fares (over 60) are automatically applied at checkout once your age is in your profile. Without a profile, you'll be quoted adult standard prices regardless of your age.

Is Eurostar Premier class worth paying for?

Only if you value the lounge access at St Pancras or Paris Nord and want a meal included. Standard class seats are comfortable — 2+2 layout, usable tables, plug sockets. The journey is 3h20 from Paris. There is no meaningful upgrade in the ride itself. Standard is fine for almost everyone.
