I Amsterdam Card 72h: Does It Actually Pay Off?

€95 for 72 hours of unlimited transport and 40+ museums. Here's the real maths before you buy.

Netherlands — Amsterdam 3 days €95 card Year-round

Every tourist information booth in Amsterdam will try to sell you the I Amsterdam Card. At €95 for 72 hours (May 2025 price), it covers unlimited GVB transport, free entry to 40+ museums, and one canal cruise. Before I bought mine, I did the maths — and I'll be honest: it depends entirely on how you actually spend your time. If you're the kind of person who hits three museums in a day and takes the tram everywhere, you'll break even by lunchtime on day one. If you're a walker who prefers one slow visit and a lot of canal-side sitting, this card will cost you more than just paying as you go. I spent 3 days in Amsterdam and I'll give you the real numbers so you can make the call yourself.

What the card actually covers

The I Amsterdam Card includes three categories: transport, museums, and one canal cruise. Here's the breakdown.

Transport: unlimited GVB in Amsterdam

Trams, buses, metro, and GVB ferries (including the free Noord ferries from behind Centraal) are all included for the duration of the card. This is genuinely useful — Amsterdam's tram network is dense and a single journey without a card costs €3.20. Two tram journeys a day for 3 days = €19.20 in transport alone. That's a material chunk of the card's value if you're moving around a lot.

Important: the card does not cover intercity NS trains. If you're day-tripping to Haarlem, Leiden, or Zandvoort, you pay separately.

Museums: 40+ included

Important

The Van Gogh Museum and the Anne Frank House are the two most-visited museums in Amsterdam — and both are excluded. The Anne Frank House sells out weeks in advance; book as soon as you know your travel dates. Van Gogh Museum also requires advance booking and does not accept walk-ins in peak season.

Canal cruise: 1 included

One canal cruise is included with the card. Standard canal cruise tours run about €17–22 if bought separately. The included cruise is 1 hour and departs from multiple central locations. It's worth using — the canal ring from the water is genuinely different to walking the banks, and it's already paid for.

The break-even maths

The card costs €95. Here's what it takes to get there:

Item Running total
Rijksmuseum (€22.50) €22.50
Moco Museum (€22.50) €45
Stedelijk Museum (€20) €65
Canal cruise (€18) €83
Trams — 2 journeys/day × 3 days × €3.20 €102.20

With three museums and moderate tram use, the card pays for itself with about €7 to spare. Add one more museum or a few extra tram journeys and the margin grows. The calculation is tight but real — this isn't manufactured value.

Tip

Activate the card first thing in the morning — 9:00 or at museum opening time. The 72-hour window runs from activation, not midnight. An early start on day one means you keep the full window into the morning of day four.

Who should buy it — and who shouldn't

Buy it if:

  • You're doing 3 full days and want to visit at least 3 museums from the included list
  • You're using trams regularly rather than walking everywhere
  • You want the canal cruise included without thinking about it
  • You're travelling with someone else and both buying cards — the savings multiply cleanly

Skip it if:

  • Your main plan is to walk the Jordaan, sit in the Vondelpark, and visit one or two places
  • You're primarily going to the Van Gogh Museum and Anne Frank House — neither is included
  • You're spending less than 3 full days in the city
  • You prefer walking and only take a tram occasionally — just tap your bank card with OVpay instead

The honest verdict: the I Amsterdam Card is a good deal for museum-hoppers who move around the city by tram. It's a poor deal if you're the kind of traveller who prefers one quality visit and a lot of time outside. Know which type you are before you buy.

Where to buy and practical details

Buying the card

Buy the I Amsterdam Card online at iamsterdam.com before you travel. You can also buy it at the VVV Amsterdam tourist offices at Centraal Station and Schiphol Airport, but the queue at Centraal can be long during peak season. The digital card (on your phone) is easier than the physical one — museum staff scan it directly.

What the card doesn't cover: the important gaps

Van Gogh Museum — €22, book weeks ahead

The Van Gogh Museum is excluded from every city card, by their own policy. Entry is €22 and the museum operates on timed entry slots. In April–May (tulip season), slots often sell out 3–4 weeks in advance. Book at vangoghmuseum.nl as soon as you have a date.

Anne Frank House — €16, sells out faster than anywhere

Also excluded, and also the most logistically demanding museum in Amsterdam. Timed entry tickets are released 2 months in advance and sell out within hours for peak dates (March–October). Set a reminder for 2 months before your visit date and book the moment slots open. Showing up without a ticket gets you turned away — there are no walk-in slots.

Heineken Experience, NEMO, Artis Zoo

These are also not included. The Heineken Experience (€21) and NEMO (€17.50) are popular but optional. If these are on your list, factor their cost into your break-even calculation separately.

Frequently asked questions

How much does the I Amsterdam Card 72h cost in 2025?

The I Amsterdam Card 72h costs €95 in May 2025. A 24h version is €65 and a 48h version is €80. The 72h option gives the best per-hour value if you plan to do 3 full days of museums and transport.

Does the I Amsterdam Card include the Van Gogh Museum?

No. The Van Gogh Museum (€22) and the Anne Frank House (€16) are both excluded from the I Amsterdam Card. These are the two most-visited museums in Amsterdam and both require advance booking — often several weeks ahead, especially the Anne Frank House which sells out fast.

Does the I Amsterdam Card cover GVB trams and metro?

Yes. The card includes unlimited travel on GVB trams, buses, metro, and ferries within Amsterdam for the duration. This includes the free ferries to Amsterdam Noord from behind Centraal station. It does not cover intercity trains to Haarlem, Leiden, or other cities outside Amsterdam.

When should I activate the I Amsterdam Card?

Activate it as early as possible on your first museum day — ideally at opening time (9:00 or 10:00). The 72-hour clock starts the moment you activate, not at midnight. Activating at 9:00 gives you until 9:00 on day four. Activating at 14:00 wastes half a day of transport and museum value.

Is the I Amsterdam Card worth it if I don't like museums?

No. If your Amsterdam days are mostly walking the canal rings, eating, visiting the Jordaan neighbourhood, and going to one or two places, the card won't pay off. The break-even requires at least 3 paid museums plus regular tram use. If you're a walker who uses trams occasionally, just tap your bank card at the GVB gates (OVpay) and pay per journey — it's cheaper.
