Dubrovnik as a Day Trip from Split — Is It Worth It?

4–5 hours each way by bus, €35 for the city walls, and a verdict that depends heavily on which month you go.

Croatia — Dubrovnik Day trip from Split ~€80–100 total Best: June or Sept

I went to Dubrovnik in early September from Split and I'm glad I didn't overnight there. The day trip format suited the city: I arrived, walked the walls before the coach parties showed up, ate lunch on a terrace just outside the Pile Gate, took the boat to Lokrum for two hours, spotted three Game of Thrones locations without trying, and caught the 18:00 bus back. The whole day cost me around €90. The city is architecturally impressive. I didn't feel compelled to give it more than one day. But I also went in September. If you go in August, this is a different article.

Getting there: Flixbus Split → Dubrovnik

The bus is the most practical option from Split. Trains don't go to Dubrovnik — there's no rail connection.

Operator Duration Price Notes
Flixbus 4–5 h €20–35 Book 48h ahead in summer
Arriva / local operators 4–5 h €15–25 From main bus station
Krilo fast catamaran 3 h €35–45 Seasonal; spectacular views

The bus crosses into Bosnia-Herzegovina at Neum for about 10 km — bring your passport even if you're an EU citizen, as border checks happen. Journey time varies: the 07:00 bus typically arrives around 11:30, the 08:00 around 12:30–13:00 depending on traffic. In peak summer, add 30–60 minutes for border queues.

Critical

Check the last bus back before you leave Split. In summer, the last Flixbus from Dubrovnik to Split is typically around 20:00–21:00. If you miss it, taxis cost €150–200. Always screenshot your return departure time before you arrive.

The city walls: €35 and how to do them right

The walls are 2 km long, 4–6 metres wide at the top, and take 60–90 minutes to walk at a reasonable pace. They're the defining experience of Dubrovnik — and they cost €35. That's not a typo and it's not a mistake. €35 per person, increased from €25 a few years ago. Whether that's worth it depends on how you approach it.

When to go on the walls

The walls open at 08:00. Being there at 08:00 or shortly after means you have the first section — the stretch above the Pile Gate looking toward Fort Lovrijenac — largely to yourself. By 10:30, the tour groups arrive in volume and the narrow sections become queues.

The other option is late afternoon: arriving after 17:00 gives you better light for photography and a second thinning of crowds as the day-trippers leave. The walls close at 18:30 in summer, so you have 90 minutes if you start at 17:00. That's enough.

The Fort

Fort Lovrijenac (the Red Keep exterior in Game of Thrones) is a separate fortification just outside the city walls. Entry is included in the wall ticket if you show it. Worth the 5-minute detour for the view back toward the Pile Gate.

Lokrum island: the escape hatch

If the old town gets to you — and in summer, it will — the ferry to Lokrum runs every 30 minutes from the old harbour and takes about 15 minutes. Return ticket is around €15–20.

Lokrum is quiet by design: no permanent residents, no beach bars, no noise. There's a ruined Benedictine monastery from the 11th century, a botanical garden established in the 19th century, rocky swimming spots, and peacocks. The peacocks are not a zoo exhibit — they simply live there and walk among visitors with complete indifference.

I'd recommend 2 hours on Lokrum rather than spending that time buying a €6 coffee on the Stradun. The shade, the sea access, and the change of pace are worth the ferry cost.

Game of Thrones locations

I'm going to give you the useful information here without the tour-guide framing. You don't need a GoT tour. All the main locations are within the old town, easy to find on foot.

  • Pile Gate — The main entrance to the old town. Filmed as the entrance to King's Landing in multiple seasons.
  • Fort Lovrijenac — Used as the exterior of the Red Keep. 5 minutes from Pile Gate, included in wall ticket.
  • Jesuit Staircase (Uz Jezuite) — The wide baroque staircase used for Cersei's Walk of Shame. 3 minutes' walk from Stradun, near the Church of St Ignatius.
  • Stradun (Placa) — The main street, used in multiple crowd scenes.
  • St Dominic Street — Behind the Dominican monastery, used as Littlefinger's brothel exterior.

The tourist office at Pile Gate has a free printed map with all locations marked. Pick one up and skip the €30 guided tour.

Honest verdict: August vs. June/September

My take

In August, Dubrovnik is overrun. The city itself is not the problem — the architecture is genuinely impressive, the setting is among the best in Europe, and the walls are worth walking once. But in August you're sharing the Stradun with cruise ship passengers in groups of 50, the restaurants inside the walls are tourist traps charging €30+ for average food, and the walls themselves become a slow shuffle rather than a walk.

In June or September, the same city with roughly a third of the people is a different experience. Restaurant prices outside the walls (the Gruž neighbourhood, or anything 10 minutes' walk from Pile Gate) drop noticeably. The walls take 75 minutes instead of 2 hours. Lokrum is quiet. If you have any flexibility on timing, go in June or September.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Split to Dubrovnik by bus?

Flixbus and local operator Arriva both run Split–Dubrovnik. Journey time is 4–5 hours depending on traffic and whether the bus crosses into Bosnia-Herzegovina at Neum. Book at least 48 hours in advance in summer — the early morning departures sell out. Price: approximately €20–35 each way.

Is it worth paying €35 for the Dubrovnik city walls?

Yes, once. The 2 km walk takes 60–90 minutes and gives you views of the old town and sea that you can't get from street level. Go at opening time (08:00) or after 17:00 when crowds are thinner. If you've done the walls before, skip them and spend the money on a boat to Lokrum.

What is Lokrum island and how do I get there?

Lokrum is a small island 600 metres offshore from the old town. Ferries run every 30 minutes from the old harbour (return ticket around €15–20). The island has a botanical garden, a ruined monastery, rocky swimming spots, shade, and peacocks. It's a good half-day alternative to the city crowds.

Which Game of Thrones locations can I see in Dubrovnik?

The main filming locations are: Pile Gate (King's Landing entrance), Stradun (multiple scenes), Fort Lovrijenac (Red Keep exterior, included with wall ticket), and Jesuit Staircase (the Walk of Shame). All are within walking distance. Pick up a free map from the tourist office at Pile Gate.

Is Dubrovnik overrated?

Partially, in August. The architecture is genuinely impressive and the setting above the Adriatic is hard to argue with. But in July and August it receives thousands of cruise ship passengers daily. The same city in June or September is a noticeably better experience: same architecture, a third of the people, lower restaurant prices outside the walls.
